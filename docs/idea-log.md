# Initial IT Venture Idea Log

## Idea 1 — HallPass

**Problem Area:**
International students do not know where to find honest reviews about dorms and cafeterias. They also find it hard to know basic things like where to eat, what the dorms are like, and how to get around campus when they first arrive.

**Target Users:**
International students starting university for the first time.

**Current Alternative:**
Students usually ask friends, search on Google, or look at the university website. However, these sources are not always honest, up to date, or written by real students.

**Proposed IT Solution:**
A website where students can read and write reviews about dorms and cafeterias. The website also has simple guides to help new students settle in, such as where to eat, what to bring, and how to find help on campus.

**Possible Technology:**
- Web application
- Cloud-based system to store reviews and user data

**Why This Is Suitable:**
International students need a simple and reliable source of information when they first arrive. A website is easy to access from any device without needing to download anything, making it ideal for students who are new to campus.

## Idea 2 — CampusMarket

**Problem Area:**
Students want to buy affordable second-hand items like textbooks, laptops, and furniture when they start university. There is no safe and simple platform just for university students to buy and sell within their campus.

**Target Users:**
All university students, especially new students and those on a tight budget.

**Current Alternative:**
Students currently use Facebook Marketplace or Carousell. However, these platforms are open to everyone and there is no way to verify that the seller is a real student, which makes it feel less safe.

**Proposed IT Solution:**
A website where university students can list and buy second-hand items from other students at the same university. Students sign up with a university email to keep it safe and trusted.

**Possible Technology:**
- Web application
- Cloud-based system to store listings and user accounts

**Why This Is Suitable:**
A student-only marketplace makes buying and selling safer and more convenient. Using a university email to sign up ensures that only real students can use the platform, which builds trust between buyers and sellers.

## Idea 3 — StudyMatch

**Problem Area:**
Students find it hard to connect with other students in the same class who want to study together. Many students end up studying alone because they do not know who to ask.

**Target Users:**
University students who want to study in a group or find project partners.

**Current Alternative:**
Students currently use LINE group chats or Discord servers. However, these require someone to set them up manually and there is no automatic way to match students based on course or schedule.

**Proposed IT Solution:**
A website where students can create a profile with their course and available study times. The website then matches them with other students who study the same subject and have a similar schedule.

**Possible Technology:**
- Web application
- Cloud-based system to store profiles and match data

**Why This Is Suitable:**
Many students want to study in groups but do not know how to find the right people. A website that automatically matches students by course and schedule saves time and makes it easier for students to connect and study together.
